# 2026-07-09 · EXPOSITORY · How a Town Plans Cooling Rooms

> 体裁: expository | 字数: ~230 | 新词: 17/17 | 语法点: 过去完成时 #10

## 短文

① A town that faces hotter summers needs an objective method for choosing where to build public cooling rooms.
② Before the council made its first draft, engineers had studied five years of hospital data and the decline in tree cover.
③ Their fundamental question was not which building looked modern, but which site could protect the largest number of residents.
④ Capacity matters because one library room may hold forty people, while a school hall has capacity equivalent to several small centres.
⑤ Planners must also consult doctors, transport staff, and housing officers before they pursue a final design.
⑥ These groups often reveal a hidden conflict between medical need and travel time.
⑦ For example, an external contractor may suggest a cheap site, but nurses may know that older residents cannot reach it.
⑧ The team can adjust the plan by adding shuttle stops or alter opening hours during dangerous afternoons.
⑨ If the building uses roof panels to generate electricity, the system can reduce costs and enable longer service during power cuts.
⑩ In some places, a safe cooling paint is treated as a chemical compound, so the owner must check whether a special licence is required.
⑪ By the time the first centre opened, the rules had evolved from a simple building project into a health plan.
⑫ The final lesson is clear: climate adaptation works best when technical choices are tied to daily routines.

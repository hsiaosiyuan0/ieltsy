# 2026-07-13 · NARRATIVE · The Market at the Old Station

> 体裁: narrative | 字数: ~250 | 新词: 17/17 | 语法点: 现在完成进行时 #13

## 短文

① For six months, Leila has been trying to transform an abandoned railway station into a weekend market for local artists.
② She has been working with a diverse team of bakers, musicians, engineers, and shopkeepers, all of whom need a flexible space.
③ Their prime concern is money: the market must earn enough revenue to keep the old building safe and the project stable.
④ At first, Leila held an abstract notion that good design simply meant placing every stall in a neat row.
⑤ Then, on a stormy Saturday, the entrance monitor showed an accurate count of visitors leaving after only ten minutes.
⑥ Amir, a psychology student, said the supposedly neutral layout ignored how colour, sound, and crowding shaped a visitor's perspective.
⑦ He had been watching families pause near the door, and he had written precise observations in the margin of Leila's floor plan.
⑧ Leila had to acknowledge that his evidence challenged her original idea, so she asked the team to modify the hall before the next market.
⑨ They moved food stalls towards the centre, created a quiet path along the wall, and left a small stage beside the waiting area.
⑩ The more flexible arrangement transformed the atmosphere: children stayed to listen, older visitors found seats, and strangers began talking.
⑪ Since that evening, vendors have been reporting higher sales, while Leila has been monitoring whether the new revenue remains stable.
⑫ She now understands that a useful notion is never merely abstract; it must be tested with accurate evidence, viewed from more than one perspective, and modified when reality disagrees.

⑤ 后来，在一个暴雨周六，入口处的监视器显示了一项准确数据：许多访客只待了十分钟便离开了。

⑩ 更灵活的布局改变了现场气氛：孩子们留下来听表演，年长访客找到了座位，陌生人也开始交谈。

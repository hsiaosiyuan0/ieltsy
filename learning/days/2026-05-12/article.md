# 2026-05-12 · ARGUMENTATIVE · Why the State Was Right to Regulate Major Art Acquisitions

> 体裁: argumentative | 字数: ~240 | 新词: 19/19 | 语法点: 一般过去时 #2

## 短文

① Last year, the British government announced new rules that regulate how national museums purchase major works of art.
② This decision drew sharp criticism, but I believe it was an appropriate step.
③ Before the reform, each institute followed its own policy, and the conduct of buyers varied widely across the country.
④ Some galleries quietly transferred public funds abroad to acquire paintings without any review.
⑤ Others commissioned new works from foreign artists, while a few constructed entire wings to display objects whose origin no one had evaluated.
⑥ Critics argued that central oversight limited artistic freedom, but they ignored one significant aspect of the problem.
⑦ Without clear rules, museums could not maintain public trust, nor could they secure long-term funding from taxpayers.
⑧ Under the new system, every purchase above one million pounds required a distinct review by an independent panel.
⑨ The panel evaluated the price, the provenance, and the cultural value of each item before approval.
⑩ Within a year, several museums obtained important pieces at fair prices, while a number of questionable acquisitions were stopped before any contract was signed.
⑪ The reform was not perfect, yet it solved a problem that the sector had ignored for decades.
⑫ Regulation, in this case, did not weaken culture — it protected it.

## 中文翻译

① 去年，英国政府宣布了新规，监管国家博物馆购买重要艺术品的方式。
② 这一决定招致尖锐批评，但我认为这是一个恰当的举措。
③ 改革之前，每家机构都遵循自己的政策，全国各地买家的行为差异很大。
④ 一些美术馆悄悄把公共资金转移到国外，去获取画作而没有任何审查。
⑤ 另一些则委托外国艺术家创作新作品，还有少数建造了整片展厅来陈列那些来源从未被评估的物品。
⑥ 批评者认为中央监督限制了艺术自由，但他们忽视了这个问题的一个重要方面。
⑦ 没有明确的规则，博物馆既无法维持公众信任，也无法从纳税人那里获得长期资金。
⑧ 在新制度下，每一笔超过一百万英镑的购买都需要一个独立专门小组进行单独审查。
⑨ 该小组在批准之前评估每件物品的价格、来源与文化价值。
⑩ 一年之内，几家博物馆以公道的价格获得了多件重要藏品，而许多可疑的收购在签订合同之前就被叫停。
⑪ 这项改革并不完美，但它解决了该行业几十年来一直忽视的问题。
⑫ 在这个例子中，监管没有削弱文化 —— 它保护了文化。

## 语法点示例

**一般过去时 · 表过去动作 / 状态**

- 句 ①: "announced ... regulate" — `announced` 是过去发生的一次性动作。
- 句 ③: "followed / varied" — 描述改革前的一段过去时间里的常态。
- 句 ④: "transferred ... to acquire" — 主动作过去时，不定式表目的。
- 句 ⑤: "commissioned / constructed / had evaluated" — 主句过去时；`had evaluated` 是过去完成时，表"在过去某动作之前"。
- 句 ⑧: "required" — 过去时间段内的一般性规定，用一般过去时。
- 句 ⑩: "obtained / were stopped" — 过去主动 vs 过去被动同时出现。
- 句 ⑪: "was / solved / had ignored" — 主句过去时 + 过去完成时（更早的"忽视"）。

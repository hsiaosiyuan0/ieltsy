# 2026-07-15 · DESCRIPTIVE · An Evening at the American History Museum

> 体裁: descriptive | 字数: ~280 | 新词: 19/19 | 语法点: `used to do` vs `be used to doing` vs `would do` #16

## 短文

① This July, the National Museum of American History in Washington is preparing for an evening event that combines a public ceremony with the museum's everyday activities.
② This evening, Melinda French Gates is scheduled to receive the Great Americans Medal for work advancing opportunity, global health, and gender equality.
③ The free, captioned, ASL-interpreted stream gives online viewers an incentive to join because neither distance nor an entrance fee stands in their way.
④ Older museum displays used to present national achievement through neat rows of famous objects; today, this federal institution incorporates live conversations and digital access into the way it tells that history.
⑤ On earlier event nights, ushers would assign seats, test microphones, and offer each guest a brief guide before the lights softened.
⑥ Staff are now used to guiding both in-person and remote audiences, and they are capable of switching calmly between stage cues and accessibility requests.
⑦ Near the theater, a digital index points toward collections donated by an artist's estate, while a small display connects personal objects with public memory.
⑧ Researchers edit labels as evidence changes and cite each source, turning corrections into part of the museum's visible work rather than hiding them.
⑨ Warm light will enhance the medal's gold surface, but the following conversation is likely to matter more than the object itself.
⑩ Questions about gender, philanthropy, and power may create a bond between visitors who entered with very different ideas about national service.
⑪ The scheduled program will not exceed ninety minutes, yet its mix of ceremony and discussion should leave enough room for disagreement as well as praise.
⑫ Furthermore, the webcast shows how a modern American museum can preserve formal traditions while opening them to people far beyond Washington.

## 语法点示例

**`used to do` vs `be used to doing` vs `would do` ★★★ · 过去状态、过去习惯与“习惯于”**

- 句 ④: `used to present national achievement` — 表示旧式展览过去常见、现在已经改变的呈现方式。
- 句 ⑤: `would assign seats` — 表示以往活动之夜反复发生的动作；这里的 `would` 不是条件句。
- 句 ⑥: `are now used to guiding` — `be used to` 表示“习惯于”，`to` 是介词，所以后接动名词 `guiding`。
